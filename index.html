Jakob Troidl

I am a Ph. D. candidate in computer science at Harvard University, advised by Hanspeter Pfister. I am broadly interested in data visualization and inverse graphics, especially with applications in computational neuroscience. In particular, my research focuses on building scalable visual analysis tools and machine-learning algorithms to analyze the hidden architecture of the brain.

I hold a M. Sc. in visual computing from TU Wien, Austria and a B. Sc. (with Honors) in medical informatics from the same university. I was born and raised in the beautiful town of Regensburg in Germany. On the side, I am a member of beetsbook.club.
